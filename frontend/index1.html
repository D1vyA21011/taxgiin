Frequently Asked Question's

* Individual Income Tax Returns (ITR) and Finance FAQs:

An ITR is a form used by individuals to report their income, deductions, and tax liabilities to the government.

Anyone with taxable income, including salary, investments, or business income, should file an ITR.

The deadline typically falls on July 31st for individuals, but it may vary based on the assessment year.

Commonly required documents include PAN card, Aadhar card, Form 16, bank statements, and investment details.

You can file ITR online through the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal or by using tax filing software.

Common deductions include HRA, Section 80C, Section 80D, and more.

Advance tax is paid periodically throughout the year by individuals with substantial tax liabilities.

* Business-Related FAQs:

GST is an indirect tax levied on the supply of goods and services in India.

GST returns are typically filed monthly, quarterly, or annually, depending on the business's turnover.

An audit service involves a detailed examination of a business's financial records to ensure accuracy and compliance with laws and regulations.

Business ITR is essential to report income, expenses, and taxes accurately and to comply with tax laws.

Business ITR includes details of business income, expenses, and deductions, while individual ITR focuses on personal income sources.

Businesses can optimize taxes through proper record-keeping, claiming eligible deductions, and strategic tax planning.

The process involves submitting an application through the GST portal, followed by verification and issuance of a GSTIN.
